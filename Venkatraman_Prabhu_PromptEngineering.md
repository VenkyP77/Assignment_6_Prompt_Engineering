# 2. What Is a Neural Network?

## A Simple Definition

A Neural Network is a group of connected computer helpers that learn from examples and work together to make decisions.

Scientists created neural networks after studying how the human brain learns.

### Important Word

**Neuron**

A neuron is a tiny cell in your brain that helps you think and learn.

Your brain contains billions of neurons.

Neural Networks use simplified computer versions of these neurons.

Don't worry about remembering the word "neuron."

For now, think of neurons as:

🧩 Tiny puzzle solvers.

### 1.3 Role and Context Analysis
Across all three scenarios, assigning a specific role (e.g., senior teacher, marketing specialist) gave the LLM a clear lens through which to calibrate tone, depth, and expertise level — eliminating guesswork that led to generic or unfocused responses. Layering in structured context — such as the audience's age and curiosity level, the interpersonal dynamics behind a job offer decision, or a brand's manufacturing philosophy — allowed the model to move from broadly plausible answers to precisely tailored, high-quality outputs that required minimal human editing. The progression from V1 to V3 consistently showed that the more specific the constraints and background provided, the less the LLM "hallucinated" intent, with the structured tag-based format in V3 virtually eliminating ambiguity and producing outputs ready for real-world use. In short, role set the voice, context set the boundaries, and together they transformed the LLM from a generalist guesser into a specialist executor.
